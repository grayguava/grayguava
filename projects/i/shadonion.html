Shadonion — Privacy & OPSEC knowledge base

A static, privacy-first website explaining digital privacy and OPSEC without hype, tracking, or corporate framing.

Project created on: 23 Jul. 2025 | Last updated: 28 Dec. 2025 | Status: Archived

Shadonion is a content-focused privacy site built for people who want clarity instead of slogans and grounded explanations instead of influencer-tier advice. It is intentionally static, readable, and inspectable in plain text.

The project exists to explain how tracking, profiling, and digital exposure actually work — without pretending tools are magic or that reading alone makes anyone private.

There is no analytics, no telemetry, no JavaScript bloat, and no attempt to turn privacy into a product. This is education and perspective, not a service.

Overview

Problem

Most privacy resources are either oversimplified marketing or fear-driven paranoia that skips real-world trade-offs and threat awareness.

Solution

A static site that explains privacy and OPSEC concepts directly, without assuming blind trust in tools, vendors, or infrastructure.

Core stack

HTML5 · CSS3 · No JavaScript frameworks · No analytics · No cookies · No telemetry

Scope & assumptions

Shadonion deliberately avoids making guarantees. It assumes readers want less tracking and better decision-making — not magical invisibility.

  • Not a Tool: This project does not provide anonymity infrastructure.
  • Not Tor: The name is thematic, not technical.
  • Not a Shield: Reading about privacy does not make you private.
  • User Responsibility: Correct application of tools is always on the user.
  • Threat Awareness: Encourages thinking before tool selection.

Content coverage

Web & browser privacy

Tracking, fingerprinting, profiling, and modern web exposure.

Basic OPSEC

Practical threat awareness without paranoia cosplay.

Mobile privacy

Phone-level risks, OS limitations, and realistic expectations.

Offline practices

Data hygiene, storage habits, and non-digital risks.

Trade-offs

Clear discussion of compromises instead of absolutist claims.
